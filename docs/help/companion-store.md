# Companion Store (Confinement)

The Companion Store — the Confinement — is where you **buy and sell companions**
(monsters that fight at your side) and manage the ones you own.

[Back to Help index](index.md)

## How to play

The screen works like the [General Store](general-store.md), with a buy list, a
detail panel, and a sell list — but the goods are creatures, not equipment.

- **Buy a companion.** Pick a monster from the stock to recruit it as an ally. Each
  stocked creature-and-alignment bucket is listed separately, and prices scale
  with how powerful the creature is. You can field at most **four companions** at
  once: purchased creatures, monsters you've charmed in the dungeon, and any
  carried dead companion body share that limit. A Tavern recruit is separate and
  does not use these slots, so buy with your roster in mind.
- **Sell a companion.** Hand a companion you own back to the Confinement for gold.
- **Bind.** Pay to raise a companion's **bind level** by 4, up to a maximum of
  12, strengthening your hold on it so it's less likely to slip away from you. A
  creature that joined you of its own free will can't be bound.
- **Realign.** For a fully identified companion, pay to change its **alignment**
  (Good, Neutral, or Evil) — useful for matching an ally to your hero.
- **Identify.** Pay to reveal more about a companion you don't fully know yet.

Newly bought companions are already fully identified. Companions recruited or
charmed in the dungeon can be only partly known, so identify those if you want
the full picture.

Your companions act on their own in combat.

## Differences from the original

- **A four-companion limit.** Because the remake gives you a single hero rather
  than a four-character party, your purchased and charmed companions — plus any
  carried dead companion body — share four companion slots. A Tavern recruit
  remains separate.

The stock, pricing, binding, realigning, and identification all follow the
original game's confinement rules.
