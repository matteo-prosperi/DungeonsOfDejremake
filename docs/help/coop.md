# Couch Co-op

Couch co-op lets two players adventure together on one machine, each controlling
their own hero. It's an optional, local two-player mode unique to this remake.

[Back to Help index](index.md)

## How to play

- Pair two independent characters into a single local session. Each player drives
  their own input device — gamepad, or keyboard + mouse on PC.
- Both heroes explore the dungeon together. You'll keep each other alive, combine
  your spells and abilities, and split the workload of fighting and looting.
- **You stay on the same floor.** The game keeps both players together across
  level changes, so you can't get stranded on different floors. Staircases and
  deliberate chutes work when you're in the same room and move both of you
  together.
- **Helpful spells reach your partner.** Beneficial party spells can target the
  other player; healing, cure, and raise spells can also target companions when
  the spell allows it.
- **Trading.** You can hand eligible unequipped items to each other through a
  dedicated trade screen, letting one player pass consumables or finds to the
  other.
- Some special tiles (teleporters and chutes) behave differently when you're apart,
  to keep you on the same floor — chutes become same-floor falls, and teleporters
  stay on your current floor instead of separating you across levels.

**Tips:**

- Build complementary heroes — a sturdy fighter plus a healer or caster covers far
  more situations than two of the same.
- Stick together for stairs and to make the most of shared healing and buffs.
- Steer monster attention on purpose: a sturdy hero can **Tank** to soak hits
  while a fragile caster keeps the **Defend** stance to stay low-priority.

## Differences from the original

- **Co-op is entirely new.** The original game was strictly single-player. Local
  two-player couch co-op is a remake-only feature, so its rules have no
  original-game equivalent:
  - the two of you are kept on the **same floor**, with teleporters and chutes
    adjusted so neither player is sent to a different level;
  - **beneficial spells can target the other player**, and healing/cure/raise
    spells can target companions when the spell allows it;
  - a dedicated **trade** screen lets you exchange eligible unequipped items;
  - **monsters pick which of you to attack based on your current action, not your
    position in the party.** The original weighted targeting by marching-order
    position; here each player's stance this round sets how much monster attention
    they draw — Tank pulls the most, then Attack, then opening a chest, then
    casting or using an item, with Defend drawing the least. This lets you
    deliberately steer hits onto a sturdier hero and treats both players
    symmetrically;
  - each player keeps their own independent character save — co-op is a runtime
    pairing, not a merged party.
- Single-player is unaffected: all of these rules only apply when you're playing
  co-op.
