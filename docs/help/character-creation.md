# Creating a Character

The character-creation screen is where every adventure begins. You build one hero
and take them all the way.

[Back to Help index](index.md)

## How to play

You'll make five choices, in roughly this order:

1. **Race.** Your race sets the minimum and maximum for each of your six stats,
   which alignments you may choose, and your experience modifier (how quickly you
   level). The screen starts with a race already selected so you always see a
   valid build; change it freely.
2. **Stats.** Your six stats — **STR, INT, WIS, CON, CHA, DEX** — use point-buy.
   Each starts at your race's minimum + 5, then you spend that race's stat-point
   pool with the + and - buttons.
   - **STR** / **DEX**: each adds attack and defense, but only when unusually high
     or low — the neutral band is 8-12, every point above it adds +2 attack and
     +2 defense, and every point below it subtracts. DEX also helps you disarm
     chest traps.
   - **INT** / **WIS**: each uses a wider neutral band of 8-14; every point above
     it adds +1 attack/defense, and every point below it subtracts. Together they
     set your spell points (SP = INT×5 + WIS×5). Both are also required to learn
     spells, and WIS additionally helps you spot chest traps.
   - **CON**: drives your maximum HP. Only your permanent base CON counts toward
     HP — raising it (with tomes or stat potions) scales your whole HP total.
   - **CHA**: needed for charm spells, improves friendly monsters' chance to offer
     to join you as companions, and reduces the chance that friendly encounters
     turn hostile. Tavern recruiting is based on gold, not CHA.
3. **Sex.** Cosmetic; affects your portrait.
4. **Alignment.** Good, Neutral, or Evil. Your race may restrict which are
   available. Alignment affects which guilds you can join and how some items and
   monsters treat you.
5. **Name** your hero and confirm.

Everyone starts as a **Nomad at level 1**. It is your launchpad before you join a
stronger guild at the [Guild Hall](guild-hall.md).

**Tip:** think about your end goal. A melee bruiser wants high STR/DEX/CON; a
caster wants high INT/WIS (and the CON to survive). You can belong to many guilds
over time, so you don't have to decide everything now.

## Differences from the original

- **You keep a single hero.** There's no four-character party to roll up; you
  create one adventurer and later gain monster companions through friendly
  encounters or charm spells. A recruited [Tavern](tavern.md) NPC is a separate
  ally, not one of those companion slots.
- **A starter kit.** New-game characters begin with a small set of
  basic equipment to get going.
