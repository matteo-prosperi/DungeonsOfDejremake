# Tavern

The Tavern is where you recruit a single **AI-controlled NPC adventurer** — a
full, player-like ally — to travel and fight alongside your hero.

[Back to Help index](index.md)

## How to play

- The Tavern shows a few adventurers available to hire. Each is a complete
  character with their own race, guilds, stats, alignment, and equipment, tuned to
  roughly match your hero's power. The roster belongs to the visiting character
  and refreshes only after an interval of time.
- **Recruiting costs gold**, paid from your bank. If you can afford them, you can
  hire them.
- You can have **one recruited NPC ally at a time**. To hire a different one,
  **dismiss** your current ally first (you won't get the recruit cost back).
- A recruited NPC is a **separate party member**, not a companion. You can keep a
  full set of up to four [companions](companion-store.md) (charmed monsters and
  monsters bought at the Confinement) *and* a recruited NPC at the same time.
- In the dungeon your NPC ally **acts on its own** — attacking, defending,
  casting, and supporting through its combat AI. You don't issue them per-action
  orders.
- **Your ally shares the purse.** A living NPC ally pulls its own weight in the
  party's economy by taking a proportional cut. While it's with you, the gold you
  collect from monsters and chests and the price you get for **selling** items are
  reduced by its share, and it likewise covers its share of costs, so
  **identification fees are lower**.
- **Your ally can get separated and lost.** Some dungeon events scatter your hero
  away from your ally, leaving them behind in the maze.
  A lost ally isn't gone for good. It rejoins you automatically the next time you
  **return to town**, or immediately if you **walk back onto the exact cell** where
  it was left. The **Soul Search** spell points you toward a lost ally, and
  **Retrieve Soul** summons it straight back to you. (Your monster **companions**
  are never separated — only the recruited NPC can be left behind.)

**Tips:**

- Pick an ally that complements your hero — a caster or healer covers gaps a
  melee build can't, and vice versa.
- Your NPC can be hurt or fall in battle; if it dies you carry its body back to
  town and it is revived there.

## Differences from the original

- **The Tavern is a remake-only feature.** The original game had no tavern and no
  way to hire a player-like ally — it was strictly a single character. Recruiting
  an autonomous NPC adventurer is new here.
- **Hiring is gold-based.** You simply pay a gold price (drawn from the ally's
  gear) from your bank; there's no Charisma check or chance of refusal. (Charisma
  instead matters for **charming monsters** in the dungeon to join you as
  companions — see [The Dungeon](dungeon.md).)
- **One NPC ally, plus your companions.** The remake keeps the recruited NPC
  separate from the up to four monster companions (charmed monsters and
  Confinement hires), which are managed separately at the
  [Confinement](companion-store.md).
